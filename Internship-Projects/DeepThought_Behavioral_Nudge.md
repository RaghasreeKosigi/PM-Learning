# Behavioral Nudge Assignment – DeepThought Growth OS
**Author:** Raghasree Kosigi  
**Nudge Title / Use Case:** Confidence Catalyst for New Team Members

---

## Overview
Designed a behavioral nudge to encourage new team members to confidently participate in team discussions.  
**Objective:** Improve psychological safety and reduce hesitation for early contribution.

---

## Problem Statement
New team members often hesitate to share ideas in discussions, remaining silent due to:  
- Social comparison  
- Fear of judgment  
- Low psychological safety  

**Impact:** Faster integration, better collaboration, increased confidence, and a more expressive team culture.

---

## Nudge Design

**Trigger:** User browsing discussions >10 minutes without contributing.  
**Inferred State:** Hesitation, low confidence, fear of being wrong.  
**Message:** “2 teammates shared their ideas this hour. Your perspective could enrich the conversation—want to share it?”  
**Delivery:** Peer-triggered dashboard prompt, supportive tone, non-intrusive.

---

## Behavioral Test Scenarios

| Case      | Likely Reaction                                   | Adjustment |
|-----------|---------------------------|-----------------------|
| Receptive | Already planning to share | Contributes naturally | No change |
| Resistant | Feels insecure | Ignores or delays                | Softer message: “Whenever you feel ready…” |

---

## Key Learnings
- Timing and tone are crucial for effective nudges  
- Subtle, psychologically informed interventions increase engagement  
- Designing for different user behaviors improves adoption  
- Enhances team psychological safety and confidence
